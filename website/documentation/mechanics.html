Mechanics

The numbers behind hunger, bonds, breeding, and money — for anyone who wants to know exactly why a fish did what it did.

Hunger

Hunger climbs on a deliberately slow clock, separate from the per-frame movement update. A fish that goes long enough without reaching food starts losing health from starvation instead — a fish can genuinely die this way.

Feeding & treats

Fish Food is bulk and impersonal — buy a pack, drop it in the water, and whichever fish reaches it first eats it. Treats work differently: bought one pack at a time from the Shop, then fed directly to one chosen fish from its own Inspector.

A treat relieves hunger by exactly the same amount as regular Fish Food — there's no bigger number for buying one, on purpose. What changes is the reaction: a species with a declared favorite (today, just the Axolotl — Brine Shrimp, Bloodworms, and Worms) lights up when fed one, shown as a nicer toast. Pizza sits outside that system entirely — nobody has it as a declared favorite, yet every fish is delighted by it regardless, which is exactly why it's kept to a single serving per purchase instead of a pack of five like the others.

Relationships

Every pair of fish shares one continuous bond score from −100 to 100, nudged by real events — waking each other, sleeping side by side, giving up a bed — and slowly decaying if left alone. The score maps to a state:

ScoreState
≤ −50😠 Rival
−49 to −15😒 Dislikes
−14 to 14Neutral
15 to 49🙂 Friend
≥ 50❤️ Best Friend

Each pair remembers up to 5 specific reasons for their score, oldest dropped first once that fills up. A Playful fish gets an extra boost to the score when it successfully wakes a friend up.

Sleep & wake-ups

About 1 in 5 fish (20%) are born Sleepy, independent of their personality. Everyone else wakes together the instant Night ends — a Sleepy fish genuinely doesn't: it stays asleep past the normal transition until a real wake attempt succeeds, resisting each one roughly 7 times out of 10. It's never permanently stuck, though — only a same-container Friend or Neutral tankmate ever bothers trying (a Rival won't), and each one is given a randomized number of chances before success becomes guaranteed: 3–5 for a Friend, 4–6 for a Neutral acquaintance. With nobody eligible in the room at all, a fallback timer wakes it anyway after about a minute.

A woken fish doesn't vanish the instant it wakes, either — it lingers a few seconds, and the whole room leaves together once every occupant is awake, not one at a time. See Decorations for the "Enter" view this all plays out in.

Breeding

Once a day, every mutual, grown-up, non-predator Friend or Best Friend pair has a 25% chance to have a baby, up to a cap of 30 total fish in the tank so it can't run away forever.

Economy

The tank's total attractiveness comes from its fish and decorations: +5 per fish (+15 instead for a "rare" species — anything priced at $50 or more, which today just means the Betta and the Shark), plus a flat +10 if there's no food currently sitting uneaten. Visitor count is attractiveness ÷ 20; each visitor's $2 ticket is settled once a day along with a flat, unconditional $10/day "Aquarium Maintenance Grant." Donations ($1–3 per visitor) don't wait for that — the moment a visitor actually donates, the money lands immediately and a toast announces it ("A visitor donated $2!"), rather than being bundled into the day-end total.

Selling a decoration refunds half its purchase price.

If the tank goes fully bankrupt — $0, no food, no fish — Emergency Aquarium Welfare (on by default, toggleable in Settings) grants a fresh start: +1 fish, +25 food, +$20.

Day & night

Water temperature and light drift together on one continuous curve, warmest at noon and coldest at midnight. Fish move measurably slower once the water turns cold, and non-hungry fish stop swimming and sleep once night falls — a hard stop, not just a slowdown.
